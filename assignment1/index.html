Questions & Answers

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience.

The palat2023 website immediately catching the eye are the vibrant, neon colors and dynamic design.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

Move the mouse around the page to see how the colors and typography change.

Click on different areas.

Scroll up and down to check if the layout or content shifts with scrolling.

Hover over specific text or patterns to note any changes or animations.

Take note of the transitions between color schemes and how smooth they are.

Read the introduction text and observe how the design complements the content.

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent most of my time exploring how the colors and text changed when I moved the mouse.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action is moving my mouse around the page. Mouse movements constantly respond to the design, making it impossible not to explore each

interaction.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal of the interactive experience seems to be to engage users through responding to mouse movements with effects like splitting images and

reversing text color.

6) How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The splitting images, reversed text, and dynamic visual effects directly interact with users, making the experience feel lively and immersive. These features

highlight the designer's creativity and technical ability, effectively conveying the intention to impress and captivate.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

The experience feels designed for short, repeated interactions. While users might spend a few minutes exploring the dynamic effects, the engaging visuals keep

them coming back.

8) How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

Providing smooth and varied interactions on each page. As users navigate through different pages, they can discover unique interactive effects, which keeps the

experience fresh and engaging. Additionally, the presence of additional links on the last page further invites users to explore the site multiple times, suggesting

that each visit may reveal new elements

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference

Digital art, graphic design, interactive installation:The dynamic visual effects and vibrant color schemes.The use of typography, layout principles.

Also the experience parallels the physical interactive art installations, where user actions directly influence the environment or artwork.

10) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

These media forms often encourage active participation and experimentation instead of passive consumption. This implies that users should be open to interacting

with the experience multiple times, trying different actions, and paying attention to how the interactions influence the outcome.

11) What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

Intrigued, curious, and creatively engaged. A sense of exploration and discovery, encouraging users to feel excited and inspired.

12) What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

The lack of clickable elements is frustrating. The dynamic visuals and responsive mouse interactions set the expectation for further exploration or pop-ups

through clicks. However, the limited clickable options make the experience feel somewhat constrained and incomplete, as if it falls short of its potential for

deeper interaction.

13) What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying part of the interaction is the bright and neon colors of the initial page. They immediately grabbed my attention. And coupled with the mouse

interactivity, which makes the experience feel lively and captivating, keeping me intrigued from the start.
